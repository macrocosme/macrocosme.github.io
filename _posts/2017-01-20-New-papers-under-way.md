2017 kicked in. Postdoc application season seems to be over. 
I therefore find myself with having some extra time to work on writing papers, along other general duties 
(I accepted to help with the [2017 astroinformatics school](http://anita.edu.au/2016/12/09/anita2017-and-astroinformatics-school-registrations-now-open/)). 
I am currently working on two in parallel: one is, in some sense, 
an extension to the [Vohl, Fluke & Vernardos paper](http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2015A%26C....12..200V); 
the other one is extending work that [I presented in Rome last September](https://speakerdeck.com/macrocosme/colouring-redshift-and-velocity-a-novel-visual-cue-to-inspect-volumetric-data-in-astronomy). 

I am obviously biased, but I have a gut feeling these two papers will be quite interesting. And, the more I write, 
the more I realise I enjoy writing, putting figures together, thinking about the overall 
look of the paper, etc. In addition to good content (of course), I do enjoy a good looking paper.  

More to come soon(ish).
