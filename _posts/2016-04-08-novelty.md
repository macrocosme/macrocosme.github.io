title: Novelty
---

Many would argue that a PhD (and possibly research more generally) is the perfect place for competent people
to feel incompetent on a daily basis. One simply needs to check [PhD comics](http://phdcomics.com/comics.php)
to get convinced. As science is based on a natural self-doubt -- where everything needs to be explained,
rethinked many times, and backed-up with references -- it is extremely easy to think that what we do is beneath
our own high standards.

I think that it is especially true when we work in a multi-disciplinary field, on the edge of different disciplines,
or in an emerging field. In these niches, finding clear references to what we discuss can be challenging,
as there may only be a handful. This can lead to thinking that what we do is not as interesting as those fields with
a decades if not centuries of litterature to dig into and build upon.

However, I like to think that if no one has done what you are presenting to the world before you,
there is probably some novelty involved.
