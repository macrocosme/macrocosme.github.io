 I have been working on an article for a current project for a few weeks now. Writing takes time. And effort.
As much as it seems straight forward when you start a paper, it always takes way more time to actually get to a
polished result than expected. And for good reasons. This in principle will last in the science archive forever.
It may be worth taking a few more reads before publishing it!

 Another point that makes writing a scientific paper a long endeavour is the documentation phase. I personally like to
  look at what has been published prior to my work (and obviously is a pre-requisite for any scientific paper anyways!)
  This means that not only do you need to cite those papers, but read them as well! And as funny as it may sound, it is
  quite easy to simply skim through a paper to find something that is of interest to the context of your work without
  grasping all that it actually includes.

 As I wrote a few papers already, it seems like it is getting a bit more of a natural process to put into place.
 Nevertheless, it still is a hard process that demands both a lot of time and mental efforts!
