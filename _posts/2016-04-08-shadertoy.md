While reading on the topic of [shader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shader) in the past weeks, I discovered a fun place on the web :
[Shadertoy](https://www.shadertoy.com). Put simply, a shader is a program that tells how to
display something on your screen.

Shadertoy lets you develop shaders online, and render in real-time using webgl.

 I made this as a test:

<iframe width="640" height="360" frameborder="0" src="https://www.shadertoy.com/embed/lstSWN?gui=true&t=10&paused=true&muted=false" allowfullscreen></iframe>

It is the [Mandelbrot set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandelbrot_set) computed in realtime, mixed with some time parameters.
The code can be seen [here](https://www.shadertoy.com/view/lstSWN). Fun times.
