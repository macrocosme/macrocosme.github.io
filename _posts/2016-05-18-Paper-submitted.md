I just submitted our latest paper to the new PeerJ journal on Computer Science (a.k.a [PeerJ Computer Science](https://peerj.com/computer-science/)!)
The paper discusses a qualitative, quantitative and comparative visualisation system we developed for multidimensional data
(e.g. a special case being 3D data). Multidimensional data is quite common in science. For instance,
magnetic resonance imaging can be visualised in 3D. Astronomers often use spectral data cubes to study the universe.

We implemented the system primarily for use in the [CAVE2 at Monash University](https://monash.edu/mivp).
This place is awesome. I'm biased, of course, but I really do think so. I like to say that it feels a bit
like the future when you walk in there. Or as Steve Crawford puts it:
["#OMG, #allthepixels, #mindblown"](http://stevecrawford.saao.ac.za/2015/10/29/adass-day-3/)!

I'm looking forward to discuss more about it here! In the meantime, let's hope the review process goes smoothly!
