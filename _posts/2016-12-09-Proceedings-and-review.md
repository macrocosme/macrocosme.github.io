Within the Centre for Astrophysics & Supercomputing at Swinburne University of Technology, 
the PhD process is ponctuated by a number of reviews: Confirmation review (~6-month), 
Mid-candidature review (~18-month), Pre-submission Review (~30-month review). Each review is done 
in two stages: writing a review documents of variable length depending on the reviewing stage to be 
sent to your evaluation panel, and a 25-30 minutes oral presentation in front of everyone in the Centre. 
The oral presentation is followed by a period of questions. Then, the panel deliberates, meets with your
advisors, and finally, meets with you. 

I completed my Pre-submission review yesterday. I am quite glad it is now behind me.
