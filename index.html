Preamble

Below is a list of scientific articles and related publications I have either written or co-authored. The research I do generally involves astronomy and computing, but has so far also extended to biological imaging, ecology, telecommunication, information theory, analytics, and data visualisation. While the work presented in these articles is either theoretical or computational, it often involves putting it to the test on advanced computing infrastructures, such as supercomputers and advanced visualisation facilities.

Since September 2018, I joined a team of astronomers and engineers to work on an astronomical survey called ALERT searching for Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) with the Westerbork Synthesis Radio Telescope (WSRT) and the International Low-Frequency Array (LOFAR) located in the Netherlands. FRBs are millisecond-duration, highly energetic, dispersed radio pulses emitted from distant regions of spacetime, far beyond the vicinity of the Milky Way. FRBs have represented the source of highly discussed open questions among astronomy academics for a little over a decade.

For my Ph.D. thesis, I addressed fundamental problems facing Astronomy in the “Big Data” era relating to processing, visualisation, analysis, storage, and transfer of data. Many planned or ongoing projects aim at increasing the number of objects studied by several orders of magnitude. As both the volume and velocity at which data is generated increase, novel methods and analytical tools are required to help understand information hidden within data. The research investigated and introduced novel visualisation methods for real-time collaborative visual analytics in immersive environments; and explored data compression to accelerate data transfer within geographically dispersed, time-critical observing programs.

Academia provides the opportunity to present this work at different conferences around the world. A list of presentations I made in different formats is included on this page.

Finally, a core part of my work has involved software development, and sometimes software training. A list of projects I worked on in the past years can also be found below.

In the hope that this page can be useful to some of you.
All the best,
- D.V.
